Philosophy Statement

One of the valuable takeaways from this difficult, global pandemic academic year is that it helped many focus on those elements that are the most important. For me to be a successful educator one needs to:

  • to create a positive class community and opportunities for collaboration
  • to encourage patient problem solving and learning from mistakes as an important part of the learning process
  • to use technology in meaningful ways

I believe that building a welcoming, supportive class community is the first step in any learning. Wondrous things can happen if time is taken to establish this first. Students are more invested and learn from teachers and fellow classmates that they believe truly care about them and their learning.

"Every child deserves a champion: an adult who will never give up on them, who understands the power of connection and insists they become the best that they can possibly be." - Rita Pierson, educator

Collaboration is an significant skill for today's workplace and the future of our planet. It is essential that we teach and foster collaboration among our young learners.

We give our students too many multiple choice assessments and in life, the big questions never have multiple choice answers if they have answers at all. We need to produce more patient problem solvers.

We need to create learning environments where students aren't afraid to make mistakes, but instead work to discover what can be learned from them.

Having access to an amazing amount of technological resources is a wonderful benefit of teaching today, but we need to remember to choose carefully.

I believe that technology use should be meaningful and should only be a replacement for other types of traditional learning when it can save time, provide a deeper experience or facilitate one that can be more easily shared.
