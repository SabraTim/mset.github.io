Coursework

The bulk of the MSET program consists of a prescribed list of common core and required courses. Students are also allowed to choose seven credit hours of courses that interest or apply to them and their specific field.

The required courses provided me with a strong overview of the education technology dicipline. Many of the projects and activities that I completed for courses were ones that I could use right away in my own classes that I was teaching. In other words, they provided very practical knowledge.

One program plus for me personally was to have the opportunity to do an independent study. MSET professors helped me coordinate with a professor from the computer science department to set up a course exploring server side programming, a topic of great interest to me.

CET 751 Computer Hardware & Networking Essentials

Almost all of the MSET courses are offered on-line, but this photo (left) was from an on campus class that reviewed hardware and networking essentials.

I was happy to have had the class at DSU and have the hands on experience with the hardware and getting to know my classmates face-to-face. I'm also pleased, however, that DSU offers an online alternative for this course for students who are not able to come to Madison, SD, but still able be a part of the MSET program.
